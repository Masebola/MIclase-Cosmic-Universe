How It Works

📖 The Guide

Everything you need to know about navigating the Miclass Cosmic Multiverse reading roadmap.

🏗 How the Roadmap is Structured

01

Phases

Both DC and Marvel are divided into numbered Phases — roughly corresponding to real-world publishing eras. DC has 10 phases (Phase 0–9), Marvel has 12 phases (Phase 0–11, including a standalone Ultimate Universe phase).

02

Tracks Within Each Phase

Inside each phase, comics are organized by track — character families or creative territories. For DC this means tracks like "Bat-Family," "Superman & Super-Family," "Flash," "Titans & Legacy," etc. For Marvel: "X-Men," "Avengers," "Street-Level," "Cosmic," etc. You don't need to read every track — just follow the ones you care about.

03

Event Pauses

Some phases feature an ⛔ Event Pause — a crossover or crisis event that reshapes the universe. When you hit one, stop your individual tracks and read the event first, then resume. These are clearly marked at the top of each phase page.

04

Tags

Each comic has optional tags: Essential means it's a major milestone you shouldn't skip. Optional means it's good but skippable. Untagged items are solid reading that fit naturally into the era.

🧭 Navigating the Site

01

Phase Pages

From DC or Marvel main pages, click any phase card to open its dedicated page. Each phase page has a filter bar — click track names to show only the comics you want. Use the search bar to find specific titles quickly.

02

The Roster

The Roster page is your character lookup. Search by name, alias, or team. Click any character to see their essential reading list and every phase they appear in. Switch between DC and Marvel using the toggle buttons at the top.

03

Essentials

The Essentials page is for casual readers or people who just want the highlights for a specific character. Select your character and get a clean list of 4-8 essential stories — no deep-dive required. Also features curated beginner reading paths.

04

Elseworlds

The Elseworlds page covers DC's non-canon stories — Elseworlds label, Black Label prestige titles, and the current Absolute Universe. Marvel's equivalent standalones are listed on the Marvel main page. All can be read anytime, in any order.

💡 Tips for New Readers

🎯
You don't have to read everything.

The roadmap is comprehensive by design — it's a reference, not a mandatory checklist. Pick the characters and eras you care about.

🔀
You can jump in anywhere.

Most major stories are readable as standalone or near-standalone. Batman: Year One doesn't require knowledge of Golden Age Batman. Hawkeye by Fraction needs zero Marvel context.

📍
Follow a character, not a universe.

If you love Batman, follow the Bat-Family track across DC phases. If you love Wolverine, follow him through Marvel phases. The tracks are designed to be readable as individual journeys.

⭐
Start with Essentials if overwhelmed.

The Essentials page is specifically built for this. Pick any character, get 5-8 stories, and you'll understand 80% of their history.

🌑
Elseworlds are some of the best comics ever made.

Don't skip them just because they're non-canon. All-Star Superman, Kingdom Come, Batman: White Knight, Old Man Logan, Mister Miracle — these are masterpieces.
